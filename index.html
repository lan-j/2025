International Workshop on
Fairness in Algorithmic Decision-Making for Education

to be held as part of the 26th International Conference on Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIED 2025)

26 July 2025 - Palermo, Italy (with support for remote attendance)

Workshop Aims and Scope

This workshop will take the first steps towards developing a blueprint to integrate fairness into AIED research, practice, and adoption. Starting from existing frameworks, including procedural fairness and algorithmic audits, participants will expand upon previous research and work to bring the AIED community into alignment around fairness principles, resulting in the co-creation of a document for embedding fairness into AIED research, practice, and adoption.

Designed as an interactive full-day, hybrid event, the workshop will incorporate short lightning talk presentations (submission details below), small-group discussions, and a world café session. By the end of the workshop, participants will have contributed to the groundwork for an actionable framework to ensure that fairness is consistently and effectively considered in AIED.

Workshop Topics

The workshop welcomes contributions in the form of abstracts to foster discussion around the co-creation of the fairness blueprint. The abstract topic should be related to fairness in algorithmic decision-making in education, with a focus on (but not limited to) the following areas:

  • Fairness methods design and incorporation
    • Incorporating fairness principles across an AIED system
    • Ensuring fairness across the AIED system lifecycle
    • Integrating fairness with other AIED areas to provide complementary benefits
  • Fairness auditing and evaluation
    • Algorithmic auditing procedures in the context of AIED
    • Improving fairness metrics, definitions, and evaluation approaches in AIED
    • Strengthening mechanisms for long-term adherence to fairness in AIED
  • Fairness gaps and challenges identification
    • Identifying gaps and challenges in AIED regarding fairness
    • Exploring what we can do better concerning fairness as a research community
    • Challenges to improving fairness in practice
    • Methods for addressing these challenges in adopting fairness
  • Procedural Fairness
    • Operationalizing and studying procedural fairness across an AIED system
    • Gaps in current AIED research related to procedural fairness
    • Ensuring procedural fairness in AIED by identifying and addressing practical implementation challenges
    • Topics related to procedural fairness in education

Keynote Speaker: Bingyi Han

Title: Teaching the Machine, Missing the Student? Why Fairness in AIEd Is Not Just a Technical Problem

Bio: Bingyi is a PhD researcher in the Human-Computer Interaction group and the Centre for AI and Digital Ethics at the University of Melbourne. Her research takes a human-centred approach to examine the ethical challenges of adopting AI in education, focusing on the lived experiences and perspectives of stakeholders. Her work addresses an underexplored area in the AIEd community, moving the ethical focus beyond data-centric and computational issues to consider user experiences, values, well-being, and the unintended consequences of interacting with AIEd systems. She frames ethical challenges as emerging from the dynamic, interdependent ecosystem of students, teachers, decision-makers, and AIEd technologies. Bingyi has also contributed to industry research on the ethics of emerging technologies at Alibaba's Ali Research Institute. In this keynote, she will explore the broader dimensions of fairness in AIEd, emphasising that fairness extends beyond algorithmic decision-making and data integrity. Drawing on empirical evidence, she will highlight the fairness issues that arise from students' interactions with AI in situated educational contexts and the stakeholder dynamics in the design and adoption process. Ultimately, this keynote calls for a human-centred approach to AIEd ethics that ensures procedural fairness and meaningful student participation in shaping these technologies.
